\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

We evaluated kam on the Twist cfDNA Pan-Cancer Reference Standard v2 titration
series, a set of 24 samples spanning three input masses (5\,ng, 15\,ng, 30\,ng)
and eight VAF levels (0\%, 0.001\%, 0.01\%, 0.1\%, 0.25\%, 0.5\%, 1\%, 2\%).
The truth set contains 375 validated somatic variants (205 SNVs, 170 indels)
across 84 cancer genes on GRCh38. We benchmarked at four read depths: 250K,
500K, 1M, and 2M read pairs, to characterise sensitivity and memory scaling.

% ── Molecule assembly ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Molecule assembly}

At 2M read pairs the assembly stage produced 367K--717K molecules per sample,
giving a raw-to-molecule compression ratio of 2.8--5.5:1. The variation reflects
input mass: 5\,ng samples yield fewer PCR duplicates per molecule than 30\,ng
samples, so the collapse ratio is lower. Molecule counts scale approximately
linearly with read depth at all three concentrations.

Duplex identification at 2M reads ranged from 4.6\% to 15.5\% of molecules
after fixing a duplex orientation comparison bug (Section~\ref{sec:evaluation}).
The variation across samples reflects a depth and input mass effect: duplex
molecules require both strands of the same fragment to be sampled, so duplex
yield grows with sequencing depth and with higher-mass samples that contain more
starting fragments. These rates are consistent with the 5--15\% range expected
for this chemistry.

% ── Variant detection ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Variant detection}

Figure~\ref{fig:sensitivity} shows sensitivity at 2M read pairs across the VAF
titration series for each input concentration.
At the lowest VAF levels (0.001\% and 0.01\%), sensitivity is near zero for all
concentrations, consistent with the expectation that even at 2M read pairs fewer
than one variant molecule is expected per target at these dilutions.
Sensitivity rises from 0.1\% VAF and reaches 52\%--61\% at 2\% VAF.

\begin{figure}[t]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{figures/sensitivity_vs_vaf.pdf}
  \caption{Sensitivity by VAF and input mass at 2M read pairs. Each point shows
    sensitivity against the 375-variant truth set. Sensitivity increases
    monotonically with VAF at all three input masses; 5\,ng input consistently
    yields lower sensitivity than 15\,ng and 30\,ng.}
  \label{fig:sensitivity}
\end{figure}

At 2\% VAF, the 15\,ng and 30\,ng samples achieve sensitivity of 61.3\% and
59.7\%, respectively.
The 5\,ng sample achieves 52.3\%.
The difference between concentrations is most pronounced at 0.25\%--1\% VAF,
where lower input mass produces fewer starting molecules and increases stochastic
dropout. At 0.5\% VAF, 15\,ng and 30\,ng samples reach 41.6\% and 48.5\%
sensitivity, respectively; 5\,ng drops to 18.7\%.

Precision varies with input mass and VAF (Figure~\ref{fig:precision_recall}).
At 2\% VAF, precision is 0.77 for 5\,ng, 0.71 for 30\,ng, and 0.57 for 15\,ng.
The 15\,ng samples have the highest duplex rates (up to 15.5\%) and the most
calls; the additional calls relative to the truth set are consistent with real
background biological variants in the cfDNA that are not part of the 375-variant
spike-in panel. False positives from two further sources are present: k-mer path
artefacts where alternative paths through the de Bruijn graph do not correspond
to real variants, and positions where the 100\,bp target window covers a germline
polymorphism not in the reference sequence used to build the allowlist.

% ── SNV vs indel sensitivity ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{SNV and indel sensitivity}

Figure~\ref{fig:sensitivity_by_type} shows sensitivity broken down by variant
type at 2M read pairs. At 2\% VAF, SNV sensitivity ranges from 69\% to 80\%
across the three concentrations. Indel sensitivity is lower, ranging from 32\%
to 39\%. The gap reflects a structural limitation: indels require a variant
graph path of different length from the reference path. Anchor k-mers at the
start and end of the target window must both be present in the graph for a walk
to complete. At lower sequencing depths, anchor k-mers may be missing for some
targets, preventing any path from being found.

\begin{figure}[t]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/sensitivity_by_type.pdf}
  \caption{SNV sensitivity (left) and indel sensitivity (right) by VAF and
    input mass at 2M read pairs. Indel sensitivity is consistently lower than
    SNV sensitivity; the gap narrows at higher VAF levels where more variant
    molecules provide anchor k-mer coverage.}
  \label{fig:sensitivity_by_type}
\end{figure}

% ── Effect of read depth ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Effect of read depth}

Figure~\ref{fig:compare_sensitivity} shows how sensitivity scales with read
depth from 250K to 2M pairs. Sensitivity gains are largest moving from 250K to
500K reads. Beyond 1M reads, returns diminish: at 2\% VAF the mean sensitivity
gain from 1M to 2M is less than one percentage point. This reflects two
saturation effects: first, the most abundant variant molecules are already
covered at 1M reads; second, the O($n^2$) UMI grouping step introduces
backpressure that limits effective depth (Section~\ref{sec:evaluation}).

\begin{figure}[t]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{figures/compare_sensitivity_vs_vaf.pdf}
  \caption{Overall sensitivity (mean across three concentrations) at four read
    depths. Each line represents one read depth. Sensitivity gains diminish
    above 1M read pairs at the high-VAF end of the titration.}
  \label{fig:compare_sensitivity}
\end{figure}

% ── Negative control ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Negative control}

The 0\% VAF samples produced 0--3 true positive calls and 25--55 false positive
calls per sample at 2M reads. The small number of apparent true positives in
the negative control are artefacts of the scoring: some calls share genomic
coordinates with truth variants by coincidence (e.g.\ a germline SNP at the
same position). The false positive count gives an upper bound on the background
artefact rate: roughly 30--50 per panel per sample at this depth.

% ── Runtime and memory ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Runtime and memory}

At 2M read pairs, wall-clock time ranged from 19.5\,s to 34.2\,s per sample on
a single core. Peak RSS ranged from 1.9\,GB to 2.4\,GB, with all samples well
within a 4\,GB budget. This is a substantial improvement over earlier
evaluations where certain samples at 200K reads reached 4.2\,GB; the improved
memory footprint reflects refinements to the pathfind stage path enumeration.
