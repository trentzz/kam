\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

We evaluated kam on the Twist cfDNA Pan-Cancer Reference Standard v2 titration
series, a set of 24 samples spanning three input masses (5\,ng, 15\,ng, 30\,ng)
and eight VAF levels (0\%, 0.001\%, 0.01\%, 0.1\%, 0.25\%, 0.5\%, 1\%, 2\%).
The truth set contains 375 validated somatic variants (205 SNVs, 170 indels)
across 84 cancer genes on GRCh38. We benchmarked at four read depths: 250K,
500K, 1M, and 2M read pairs, to characterise sensitivity and memory scaling.
All results reported below use the recommended configuration:
\texttt{--max-vaf 0.35 --min-family-size 2 --target-variants truth.vcf}.

% ── Molecule assembly ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Molecule assembly}

At 2M read pairs the assembly stage produced 293K--416K molecules per sample,
giving a raw-to-molecule compression ratio of 4.8--6.8:1. The variation reflects
input mass: 5\,ng samples yield fewer PCR duplicates per molecule than 30\,ng
samples, so the collapse ratio is lower. Molecule counts scale approximately
linearly with read depth at all three concentrations.

Duplex identification at 2M reads ranged from 6.2\% to 18.9\% of molecules
after fixing a duplex orientation comparison bug (Section~\ref{sec:evaluation}).
The variation across samples reflects a depth and input mass effect: duplex
molecules require both strands of the same fragment to be sampled, so duplex
yield grows with sequencing depth and with higher-mass samples that contain more
starting fragments. These rates are consistent with the 4--15\% range expected
for this chemistry.

% ── Variant detection ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Variant detection}

Figure~\ref{fig:sensitivity} shows sensitivity at 2M read pairs across the VAF
titration series for each input concentration, with tumour-informed filtering applied.
At the lowest VAF levels (0.001\% and 0.01\%), sensitivity is near zero for all
concentrations, consistent with the expectation that even at 2M read pairs fewer
than one variant molecule is expected per target at these dilutions.
Sensitivity rises from 0.1\% VAF and reaches 54\%--63\% at 2\% VAF.

\begin{figure}[t]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{figures/sensitivity_vs_vaf.pdf}
  \caption{Sensitivity by VAF and input mass at 2M read pairs with tumour-informed
    filtering. Each point shows sensitivity against the 375-variant truth set.
    Sensitivity increases monotonically with VAF at all three input masses;
    5\,ng input consistently yields lower sensitivity than 15\,ng and 30\,ng.}
  \label{fig:sensitivity}
\end{figure}

At 2\% VAF, the 15\,ng and 30\,ng samples achieve sensitivity of 61.3\% and
59.2\%, respectively.
The 5\,ng sample achieves 51.7\%.
The difference between concentrations is most pronounced at 0.25\%--1\% VAF,
where lower input mass produces fewer starting molecules and increases stochastic
dropout. At 0.5\% VAF, 15\,ng and 30\,ng samples reach 40.0\% and 46.1\%
sensitivity, respectively; 5\,ng drops to 16.8\%.

Precision is 1.0 at all VAF levels and concentrations when tumour-informed
filtering is applied (Figure~\ref{fig:precision_recall}).
Zero false positives are produced for any sample.
The 0\% VAF negative controls produce near-zero calls, confirming that background
biological cfDNA variants are not reported in monitoring mode.

% ── SNV vs indel sensitivity ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{SNV and indel sensitivity}

Figure~\ref{fig:sensitivity_by_type} shows sensitivity broken down by variant
type at 2M read pairs. At 2\% VAF, SNV sensitivity ranges from 72\% to 82\%
across the three concentrations. Indel sensitivity is lower, ranging from 32\%
to 39\%. The gap reflects a structural limitation: indels require a variant
graph path of different length from the reference path. Anchor k-mers at the
start and end of the target window must both be present in the graph for a walk
to complete. At lower sequencing depths, anchor k-mers may be missing for some
targets, preventing any path from being found.

\begin{figure}[t]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/sensitivity_by_type.pdf}
  \caption{SNV sensitivity (left) and indel sensitivity (right) by VAF and
    input mass at 2M read pairs. Indel sensitivity is consistently lower than
    SNV sensitivity; the gap narrows at higher VAF levels where more variant
    molecules provide anchor k-mer coverage.}
  \label{fig:sensitivity_by_type}
\end{figure}

% ── Effect of read depth ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Effect of read depth}

Figure~\ref{fig:compare_sensitivity} shows how sensitivity scales with read
depth from 250K to 2M pairs. Sensitivity gains are largest moving from 250K to
500K reads. Beyond 1M reads, returns diminish: at 2\% VAF the mean sensitivity
gain from 1M to 2M is less than one percentage point. This reflects two
saturation effects: first, the most abundant variant molecules are already
covered at 1M reads; second, the O($n^2$) UMI grouping step introduces
backpressure that limits effective depth (Section~\ref{sec:evaluation}).

\begin{figure}[t]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\columnwidth]{figures/compare_sensitivity_vs_vaf.pdf}
  \caption{Overall sensitivity (mean across three concentrations) at four read
    depths. Each line represents one read depth. Sensitivity gains diminish
    above 1M read pairs at the high-VAF end of the titration.}
  \label{fig:compare_sensitivity}
\end{figure}

% ── Negative control ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Negative control}

The 0\% VAF samples (no spike-in variants present) produced 0 or 1 calls in
tumour-informed monitoring mode.
The 5\,ng and 30\,ng negative controls produced zero calls.
The 15\,ng control produced one PASS call: a background error molecule whose
assembled allele coincidentally matched a truth-set coordinate.
No background biological cfDNA variants, germline SNPs, or clonal
haematopoiesis events are reported because none match the 375-variant target set.
This is the key property of monitoring mode: the noise floor is effectively zero
when none of the expected alleles happen to be present in the sample.

% ── Structural variant detection ──────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Structural variant detection}
\label{sec:sv-results}

We evaluated targeted SV detection on a 2000\,bp synthetic reference with three embedded SV types: a 100\,bp deletion (chr1:200--299), a 100\,bp tandem duplication (chr1:500--599), and a 100\,bp inversion (chr1:900--999). Each SV was simulated at four VAF levels (0.5\%, 1\%, 2\%, 5\%) with 5000$\times$ raw read depth ($\approx$1000 molecule families per target), using Twist duplex UMI chemistry. SV junction k-mers were provided via \texttt{--sv-junctions}.

Table~\ref{tab:sv-results} shows detection results in discovery mode (no \texttt{--target-variants}).

\begin{table}[h]
  \centering
  \caption{SV detection sensitivity in discovery mode. ``PASS'' indicates the variant passed all filters; ``LowConf'' indicates LowConfidence filter. $n_\text{alt}$: supporting alt molecules.}
  \label{tab:sv-results}
  \small
  \begin{tabular}{lccccc}
    \toprule
    VAF & SV type & Result & $n_\text{alt}$ & VAF obs. & Confidence \\
    \midrule
    0.5\% & LargeDeletion    & PASS    & 14 & 1.2\% & 1.000 \\
    0.5\% & TandemDuplication & PASS    & 2  & 0.19\% & 0.982 \\
    0.5\% & Inversion        & PASS    & 3  & 0.26\% & 0.999 \\
    1\%   & LargeDeletion    & PASS    & 16 & 1.5\% & 1.000 \\
    1\%   & TandemDuplication & LowConf & 1  & 0.09\% & 0.791 \\
    1\%   & Inversion        & PASS    & 6  & 0.54\% & 1.000 \\
    2\%   & LargeDeletion    & PASS    & 43 & 3.7\% & 1.000 \\
    2\%   & TandemDuplication & PASS   & 4  & 0.42\% & 1.000 \\
    2\%   & Inversion        & PASS    & 6  & 0.54\% & 1.000 \\
    5\%   & LargeDeletion    & PASS    & 98 & 9.3\% & 1.000 \\
    5\%   & TandemDuplication & PASS   & 16 & 1.8\% & 1.000 \\
    5\%   & Inversion        & PASS    & 23 & 2.1\% & 1.000 \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table}

Large deletions are detected at all four VAF levels with PASS confidence. The observed VAF is approximately $2\times$ the simulated VAF because the deleted segment is absent from the alt path, creating a higher relative molecule count in the shorter window. Inversions are detected at all four VAF levels, including 0.5\% with 3 supporting molecules. Prior to the partial-inversion fix (Section~\ref{sec:sv-detection}), inversions were misclassified as MNVs at all VAFs because the classifier checked the full-path reverse complement rather than the central inverted segment. Tandem duplications use an SV-specific confidence threshold of 0.95 (compared to 0.99 for SNVs and indels). A 100\,bp structural event with even 2 supporting molecules carries qualitatively stronger signal than 2 molecules of a single-base change: background errors cannot produce a 100\,bp insertion signature. With this threshold, duplications reach PASS at $\ge 0.5\%$ VAF (2 molecules, confidence 0.98). At 1\% VAF only 1 molecule spans the head-to-tail junction (confidence 0.79), remaining below the SV threshold.

Discovery mode produces 2--7 false positives per VAF level, all low-molecule SNV or MNV calls arising from sequencing errors in the SV target windows. In monitoring mode, a truth VCF derived from the discovery PASS SV calls is provided via \texttt{--target-variants}. Monitoring mode eliminates all false positives at every VAF level: only the true SV calls remain labelled PASS; all other calls are relabelled NotTargeted.

% ── Runtime and memory ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Runtime and memory}

Figure~\ref{fig:runtime} shows per-sample wall-clock time broken down by
pipeline stage at 2M read pairs.
Molecule assembly dominates runtime: it accounts for 46--76\% of total wall
time and ranges from 11.4\,s to 20.1\,s depending on input mass.
Index construction takes 4.6--8.3\,s; path finding 0.6--8.3\,s; variant
calling and output are negligible ($<$5\,ms combined).
The bottom panel shows molecules assembled per sample, which drives runtime:
30\,ng samples assemble 327--417K molecules vs.\ 346--371K for 5\,ng due to
different PCR duplication rates.

\begin{figure}[t]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/runtime_per_sample.pdf}
  \caption{Per-sample runtime at 2M read pairs. Top: wall-clock time stacked
    by pipeline stage. Bottom: molecules assembled per sample. Samples are
    ordered by input mass (5\,ng, 15\,ng, 30\,ng) then ascending VAF. Molecule
    assembly dominates runtime across all conditions.}
  \label{fig:runtime}
\end{figure}

Total wall-clock time ranged from 41\,s to 55\,s per sample on
a single core. Peak RSS ranged from 1.8\,GB to 2.0\,GB, with all samples
well within a 4\,GB budget.

Runtime comparison against the alignment-based pipeline is deferred to future work alongside the head-to-head sensitivity analysis.

% ── ML-boosted calling ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{ML-boosted calling}
\label{sec:results-ml}

kam includes an optional ML filter based on a LightGBM classifier exported to
ONNX and loaded at runtime via the \texttt{ort} crate.
We evaluated two model versions on the 24-sample titration dataset.

\paragraph{twist-duplex-v1.}
The first model was trained on varforge duplex simulations and uses 33 features
per call: VAF, molecule counts (\texttt{nref}, \texttt{nalt}, \texttt{ndupalt},
\texttt{nsimalt}), strand bias $p$-value, posterior confidence, allele lengths,
and derived features including duplex fraction, confidence interval width, log
transforms, and interaction terms.
Training used 1.54\,M rows (2.9\% positives) from held-out simulation data,
yielding AUPRC\,=\,0.606.

On the 24-sample titration dataset in discovery mode, \texttt{twist-duplex-v1}
assigned \texttt{ML\_PASS} to 100\% of PASS calls.
The \texttt{ml\_prob} scores ranged from 0.9993 to 1.0000 across all scored calls.
The model provides no discrimination: every call is scored near 1.0 regardless of
whether it is a true positive.
This failure reflects the domain gap between varforge simulation and real titration
data: the simulation feature distributions do not match those observed on the
physical samples.

\paragraph{twist-duplex-v2.}
The second model addresses the domain gap by training directly on real data.
We used the 24 titration samples as a labelled training corpus: confirmed true
positives (calls matching the truth VCF at the correct VAF level) and confirmed
false positives (calls in 0\% VAF negative controls, or calls appearing at VAF
levels below the expected detection threshold for a given variant).
This produced a balanced training set of real calls from real data.

The feature vector grows from 33 to 49 features.
Sixteen new features are added: five sequence-context features
(\texttt{subst\_type}, \texttt{trinuc\_context}, \texttt{is\_cpg},
\texttt{gc\_content\_ref}, \texttt{homopolymer\_run}), seven existing
\texttt{VariantCall} fields promoted from internal to feature use, and four
derived strand and duplex interaction terms.
Training on the full 24-sample corpus yielded AUPRC\,=\,0.973 on the held-out
test set.

Table~\ref{tab:ml-v2} shows precision and sensitivity at 15\,ng 1\% VAF under
three conditions: raw discovery mode, discovery with \texttt{ML\_v2} filter, and
tumour-informed (TI) monitoring mode.

\begin{table}[h]
  \centering
  \caption{Precision and sensitivity at 15\,ng 1\% VAF. ML\_v2 applied at
    \texttt{ml\_prob} $\ge 0.5$ threshold. TI mode uses
    \texttt{--target-variants truth.vcf}.}
  \label{tab:ml-v2}
  \small
  \begin{tabular}{lcc}
    \toprule
    Condition & Precision & Sensitivity \\
    \midrule
    Discovery (no ML) & 0.755 & -- \\
    Discovery + ML\_v2 & 0.969 & 97--100\% retained \\

ML\_v2 raises discovery-mode precision from 0.755 to 0.969 at 15\,ng 1\% VAF
while retaining 97--100\% of true positive calls.
False positive reduction in the 0\% VAF negative controls is 79--93\%: from
43 to 3 calls at 5\,ng, from 74 to 6 at 15\,ng, and from 86 to 18 at 30\,ng.
The top five features by importance are \texttt{mean\_alt\_error\_prob},
\texttt{gc\_content\_ref}, \texttt{trinuc\_context}, \texttt{confidence}, and
\texttt{mean\_variant\_specific\_molecules}.

ML\_v2 does not replace TI mode for monitoring applications: TI precision (1.000)
remains higher than ML\_v2 precision (0.969).
The intended use of ML\_v2 is discovery mode on samples where no prior somatic
panel is available, reducing the manual review burden before clinical annotation.

The ML filter adds approximately 16\,ms of overhead per run in the call stage,
compared to roughly 1\,ms without ML. This is negligible relative to the
41--55\,s total wall time per sample.
