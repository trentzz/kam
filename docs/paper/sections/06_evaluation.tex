\section{Evaluation}
\label{sec:evaluation}

The benchmark results reveal two remaining limitations in the current
implementation. We discuss each in turn.

% ── Duplex identification ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Duplex identification gap}
\label{sec:eval:duplex}

At 2M read pairs, duplex identification rates ranged from 2.9\% to 5.1\% of
molecules, approaching the expected 5--15\% for this chemistry and depth. The
remaining gap below the upper expected range has a known cause: the endpoint
fingerprint comparison does not canonicalise fingerprints before comparing.
The forward-strand fingerprint is compared directly against the reverse-strand
fingerprint, but the two are reverse complements of each other. The XOR Hamming
distance between a fingerprint and its reverse complement is large (typically
near the maximum for a 32-bit fingerprint), exceeding the identity threshold of
4 bits. This misclassifies some duplex pairs as unrelated, suppressing the
reported duplex rate.

The fix requires comparing the forward fingerprint against the reverse
complement of the reverse fingerprint. This is a single-line change in the
assembly stage. Once applied, duplex rates should reach the full 5--15\% range,
strengthening the confidence model and the strand bias filter.

At shallower depths (200K--500K reads) the duplex rate was near zero, not
because the bug is worse, but because duplex pairs require both strands of the
same fragment to be sampled. Duplex yield grows with depth until fragment
saturation; the 2M read results demonstrate that the core assembly logic is
correct.

% ── Sensitivity ceiling at lower depths ───────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Sensitivity at sub-0.1\% VAF}
\label{sec:eval:sensitivity}

Sensitivity is near zero at 0.001\% and 0.01\% VAF across all depths and
concentrations. At 0.01\% VAF, a 2M-read sample contains on average 2 variant
read pairs targeting each 100\,bp window. After UMI grouping and duplex
collapsing, fewer than one variant molecule is expected per target. The minimum
alt molecule threshold of 2 prevents these from passing the confidence filter.

Detecting at sub-0.1\% VAF requires either deeper sequencing or a reduced
minimum alt molecule threshold. The O($n^2$) UMI grouping step currently limits
practical depth: for 2M read pairs the peak RSS reaches 2.4\,GB; scaling
further would require switching to a hash-partition strategy (group reads by
UMI prefix, then compare within partitions), which would reduce expected
complexity to O($n$) and allow tens of millions of read pairs within a 4\,GB
budget.

% ── Overall assessment ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\subsection{Overall assessment}

At 2\% VAF with 2M reads, kam detects 56--59\% of truth variants (75--79\% of
SNVs, 32--36\% of indels) with precision of 0.72--0.84. This is a strong
baseline for an alignment-free approach on a 375-target panel.

The dominant loss factor at this depth is no longer memory: all 24 samples at
2M reads complete within 34 seconds and 2.4\,GB RSS on a single core. The
primary improvement available without algorithm changes is the duplex
orientation fix: restoring full duplex signal will raise both sensitivity (more
molecules passing the duplex evidence threshold) and precision (fewer
strand-biased artefacts).

Indel sensitivity is lower than SNV sensitivity at all VAF levels. This is a
structural characteristic of anchor-based graph walking, not a scoring bug:
when a variant path is a different length from the reference path, the end
anchor k-mer shifts position relative to the reference. Coverage of that anchor
k-mer from sequencing reads is required for a walk to complete. At 0.1\% VAF
some targets lack sufficient anchor coverage, reducing indel sensitivity more
than SNV sensitivity. Increasing depth further would partially close this gap.
