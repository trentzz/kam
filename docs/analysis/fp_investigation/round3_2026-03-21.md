# FP Investigation Round 3 — 2026-03-21

## Summary

With `--max-vaf 0.35` (v6a) applied, the focus shifts to understanding the irreducible FP
floor and to characterising the remaining precision ceiling. This round does not make additional
code changes; it characterises the remaining FP population and identifies where further
precision gains are feasible.

---

## Remaining FP Profile (v6a)

### FP floor at 0% VAF

The 0% VAF samples contain no spike-in variants. Any PASS call in these samples is a FP.

| Input mass | FP count (v6a) | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| 5ng | 50 | Low depth, fewer unique molecules, fewer background calls |
| 15ng | 86 | More input, higher duplex rate, more background cfDNA variants called |
| 30ng | 96 | Most input, highest molecule count, most background variants |

These 50–96 FPs per sample represent the background noise floor of the caller: variants called
in the cfDNA background that are real biological variants (germline heterozygous SNPs with
reduced allele balance, somatic variants in normal tissue, clonal haematopoiesis) but are not
in the Twist spike-in truth set. They are not artefacts: they pass strand bias, posterior
confidence ≥ 0.99, and VAF ≤ 0.35. Without a matched normal or population allele frequency
annotation, they cannot be distinguished from somatic calls.

### Remaining FPs at 2% VAF

At 30ng 2% VAF, v6a yields 222 TP and 82 FP (precision 0.730). After removing the ~96 0%-VAF
background calls, the net spike-in-attributable FPs are approximately zero (the FP count at
2% VAF is lower than at 0% VAF in most conditions). This means the 82 FPs at 2% VAF are
dominated by the background cfDNA variants, not by new calls introduced by the spike-in.

This is the core precision ceiling: the caller is correctly identifying all variants supported
by the evidence, but the truth set only covers 375 spike-in variants, not the full set of
variants present in the cfDNA background.

## Sensitivity Profile (v6a)

| Condition | Sensitivity | SNV sens | Indel sens |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5ng 2% VAF | 52.3% | 69.3% | 31.8% |
| 15ng 2% VAF | 61.1% | 79.5% | 38.8% |
| 30ng 2% VAF | 59.2% | 77.1% | 37.7% |

The large SNV–indel sensitivity gap (39–41pp at 30ng) is the primary remaining sensitivity
limitation. It arises from the anchor k-mer coverage requirement for indels: the end anchor
shifts by the indel length, requiring reads to cover that shifted position. At 2M reads and
0.592% expected variant molecule fraction this is not always satisfied.

At lower VAF conditions the sensitivity drop is steep:
- 30ng 0.5% VAF: 48.3% (reasonable)
- 30ng 0.25% VAF: 28.5% (stochastic dropout dominates)
- 30ng 0.1% VAF: 8.0% (fewer than 2 alt molecules per target in most cases)

---

## What Reduces the FP Floor

The 50–96 background FPs per sample cannot be reduced by post-calling filters alone without
also losing truth TPs, because the background variants and the spike-in variants are
indistinguishable by molecule count, strand bias, or posterior confidence.

Approaches that could reduce the FP floor:
1. **Population allele frequency annotation**: common germline SNPs (gnomAD AF > 0.01)
   filtered as `GermlineRisk`. This would remove most of the germline heterozygous background
   at VAF 0.3–0.5, on top of max_vaf=0.35 which already removes VAF > 0.35.
2. **Matched normal subtraction**: variants present in the normal cfDNA fraction at the same
   site subtracted from the tumour calls. Not always available in a liquid biopsy workflow.
3. **Panel of normals**: variants consistently called across multiple normal samples at a site
   flagged as a systematic artefact or common germline variant.

These are all orthogonal to the current implementation and are left for future work.

---

## What Reduces the Sensitivity Gap

The indel sensitivity gap (SNV 69–80% vs indel 32–39%) is structural:
1. **Wider anchor displacement**: for an indel of length L, the end anchor shifts L positions.
   At 2M reads with ~600K molecules, shorter indels are more easily called than longer ones.
2. **k-mer size effect**: with k=31, a 1-bp indel produces a 31-bp novel sequence context;
   a 10-bp indel produces a 41-bp novel context. Larger indels have proportionally fewer
   k-mers that uniquely identify the variant.

Potential improvements:
- Reduce k to 25 for indel-prone targets to improve anchor coverage.
- Extend graph walk tolerance: allow partial anchor matches or fuzzy end-anchor matching.
- Add local assembly: for missing end-anchor paths, extend the walk using partial overlaps.

These are medium-complexity changes. The hash-partitioned UMI grouping (replacing O(n²)) is
the prerequisite for scaling depth, which would improve both SNV and indel sensitivity.

---

## Conclusion

With max_vaf=0.35 applied (v6a), the remaining 82 FPs at 30ng/2% VAF are dominated by
real biological cfDNA background variants, not algorithm artefacts. Further precision gains
require external annotation (population AF, matched normal) rather than calling filter changes.

Sensitivity is limited by: (1) the depth ceiling from O(n²) UMI grouping; (2) the anchor
coverage requirement for indels; and (3) stochastic molecule dropout at VAF < 0.25%.

The v6a results (precision 0.730, sensitivity 0.592 at 30ng/2% VAF) represent the practical
ceiling for this depth and configuration. Scaling to 5M+ reads with hash-partitioned UMI
grouping is the highest-impact next step.
