# FP Investigation Round 2 — 2026-03-21

## Summary

Round 2 exposed `--max-vaf` and `--min-alt-molecules` as CLI flags, then benchmarked two
configurations:

- **v6a**: `--max-vaf 0.35` only (filter germline heterozygous FPs)
- **v6b**: `--max-vaf 0.35 --min-alt-molecules 3` (also raise molecule threshold)

v6a produces modest, clean precision gains with negligible TP loss. v6b yields large precision
gains at high VAF (≥1%) but severe sensitivity loss at low VAF (≤0.5%), making it unsuitable
as a general default.

---

## Code Changes

### New CLI flags in `CallArgs` and `RunArgs`

Added to `cli.rs`:
- `--max-vaf <f64>`: maximum VAF for a PASS call; calls above this are labelled HighVaf.
  Passes through to `CallerConfig::max_vaf`.
- `--min-alt-molecules <u32>`: minimum alt-supporting molecules. Overrides the default of 2.
  Passes through to `CallerConfig::min_alt_molecules`.

Both flags are optional; defaults are unchanged (`max_vaf=None`, `min_alt_molecules=2`).
The benchmark script was updated to pass these via `--max-vaf` and `--min-alt-molecules`.

v6b reduces FPs by ~45–50 at 30ng, but at the cost of significant sensitivity loss:
- At 0.1% VAF, precision drops from 0.242 to 0.170 (worse) and sensitivity drops from 8.0% to 2.7%.

- At 1–2% VAF, the trade-off improves: precision gains of 0.12–0.17 with sensitivity loss of 1.6–2.4%.

## Analysis

### Why max_vaf=0.35 removes ~8 FPs across all conditions

The ~8 FPs removed by max_vaf=0.35 at every VAF level (including 0% VAF) are germline
heterozygous variants: SNPs present in the tumour and normal cfDNA fraction, appearing at
VAF ≈ 0.5 regardless of the spike-in content. They are consistent across all 30ng samples,
which confirms their germline origin.

At 30ng 2% VAF, the remaining 82 FPs (down from 93) are low-VAF cfDNA biological variants
and artefacts not in the spike-in truth set.

### Why min_alt_molecules=3 has a bad sensitivity trade-off

At low VAF (≤ 0.5%) and moderate depth (2M reads), many truth variants have only 1–2
alt-supporting molecules per target. Raising the threshold from 2 to 3 removes a large
fraction of these low-evidence truth calls.

The break-even point appears to be around 1% VAF: above 1%, the TP loss is small (1–4%)
and the FP reduction is large (~50%). Below 0.5%, the TP loss exceeds the FP reduction in
relative terms, degrading precision rather than improving it (30ng 0.1% VAF: precision
0.242 → 0.170).

---

## Recommendation

**v6a (max_vaf=0.35)** is the right default change for ctDNA somatic variant calling:
- Clean, consistent 8–11 FP reduction across all conditions
- Negligible sensitivity loss
- Simple, interpretable filter (removes germline heterozygous variants)

**v6b (min_alt_molecules=3)** is appropriate only for high-sensitivity targeted panels at
high VAF (≥ 1%). It should not be the default. A future `--high-confidence` mode that
combines max_vaf=0.35 and min_alt_molecules=3 would be useful for users who prefer
precision over sensitivity.

## Next Steps

1. Make `max_vaf=0.35` the default for the `run` subcommand in ctDNA mode (or document
   it as the recommended flag for somatic variant calling).
2. The 0% VAF baseline still shows 96 FPs even after max_vaf filtering. These are real

   Investigate whether any additional evidence features (e.g., population allele frequency
   from gnomAD, or consistency across replicates) could reduce this floor.
3. At 30ng 2% VAF, precision is 0.730 with sensitivity 0.592. Sensitivity is limited mainly
   by indels (30ng 2% VAF: indel sensitivity = 0.202, SNV sensitivity = 0.780). Investigate
   indel false negatives as a separate track.
