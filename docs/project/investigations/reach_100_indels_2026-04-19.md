# Investigation: Pushing indel sensitivity towards 100/170

**Date:** 2026-04-19
**Area:** SNV/indel, TI mode, sensitivity
**Goal:** Detect 100/170 truth indels on 15ng 2% VAF sample, TI mode, FP unconstrained

## Background

Previous investigations established a ceiling of ~41% (72/170) indel sensitivity on the 15ng 2% VAF
1M-read subsample. Prior work confirmed that ~98/170 truth indel targets have zero alt k-mers at
1M reads, and the same at 10M reads. This investigation pushes every parameter axis to approach
100/170 and attempts to characterise the data ceiling precisely.

FPs are not a concern here because TI mode filters all non-truth calls at the scoring stage.

## Sample and setup

- Sample: 15ng 2% VAF, 1M-read subsample
  - R1: `~/tmp/kmtools_15ng_2pc/15ng_2pc_1M_R1.fastq.gz`
  - R2: `~/tmp/kmtools_15ng_2pc/15ng_2pc_1M_R2.fastq.gz`
- Truth VCF: `docs/benchmarking/01-snvindel/scripts/truth_variants.norm.vcf` (repo path)
  - 170 INDEL, 205 SNP truth variants
- kam binary: `target/release/kam` (repo path)
- Experiment root: `~/tmp/indel_push_15ng_2pc/`

## Findings

### 1. Wider windows help marginally (+1 indel)

Increasing target windows from 100bp to 150bp or 200bp recovers 1 additional indel (71 → 72).
This is a boundary case where the variant fell at the edge of the 100bp window. Beyond 150bp,
there is no further gain. This 1-indel gain is already present in the v2 batch results.

### 2. Confidence threshold and k-mer size have no effect

Setting `--min-confidence 0.0`, `--kmer-size-override 25`, and `--kmer-size-override 21` all give
identical results to the baseline. In TI mode, the tumour-informed filter controls PASS assignment
for truth variants — the confidence threshold affects non-TI calls only. The k=21 and k=25 results
confirm that shorter k-mers do not expose additional alt-molecule evidence for the missing 98 indels.
This rules out k-mer size as a limiting factor: the alt alleles simply are not in the reads at any
representable k-mer length.

### 3. Full-depth HUMID dedup gives equal or worse sensitivity

The pre-existing full FASTQ HUMID-deduped run (756,205 read pairs from 64M raw reads) gives
70/170 indels with kmtools — worse than the 1M head subsample. PCR deduplication removes more
molecules than the head subsample, leaving fewer unique molecules per target. This rules out read
depth as a limiting factor.

### 4. More reads recover 2 additional indels (72 → 74)

The 5M-read subsample recovers 74/170 indels vs 72/170 at 1M reads. Two additional variants become
detectable with 5x more reads:

- **chr10:43102447 Del(3bp):** At 1M reads this was filtered as StrandBias (p=0.0089, threshold
  0.01) with 12 alt molecules (2 duplex, 3 fwd, 7 rev). At 5M reads it is PASS with 15 alt molecules
  (3 duplex, 6 fwd, 6 rev). The strand counts balance as more reads are added, removing the spurious
  filter trigger. This confirms the 1M result was a filter artefact caused by sampling noise, not a
  true strand bias.

- **chr11:108365362 Del(3bp):** At 1M reads, jellyfish showed a single junction k-mer with count 4
  (approximately 2 alt reads, insufficient for assembly). At 5M reads, all 29 junction k-mers are
  present with count 2 each in the full reads (confirmed by `recheck_allowlist.py`). Kam assembles 1
  simplex alt molecule and the call is PASS.

### 5. Ceiling confirmed at 74/170 for this investigation

With 5M reads and 150bp targets, the ceiling is 74/170 = 43.5%. The true data ceiling (based on
junction k-mer analysis) is 76/170 at 1M reads and 79/170 at 5M+ reads. Kam at 5M achieves
74/79 = 93.7% of the achievable ceiling.

## True ceiling analysis

**Method:** Junction k-mers were computed as `kmers(alt_seq, k=31) \ kmers(ref_seq, k=31)`. A
variant is "above ceiling" if any junction k-mer is present in the raw reads with count ≥ 1 (1M
reads) or ≥ 2 (full reads, to reduce noise).

| Metric | 1M reads | 5M reads |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Variants with any junction k-mer evidence | 76/170 (44.7%) | 79/170 (46.5%) |
| Variants with only flanking k-mer evidence (no junction) | 43/170 | — |
| Variants with zero k-mer evidence | ~51/170 | ~48/170 |
| Kam actual result | 72/170 (42.4%) | 74/170 (43.5%) |
| Kam / ceiling ratio | 72/76 = 94.7% | 74/79 = 93.7% |

The 43 "flanking-only" variants in the jellyfish data have k-mers that are shared with the
reference sequence. These inflate naive ceiling estimates — they have no junction-specific signal
and cannot be detected by any assembly-based caller.

## Root cause analysis

The 96 missing indels at 5M reads are absent from the ctDNA dilution sample. Their alt alleles are
not represented in the sequencing data at any tested depth (1M, 5M, full FASTQ ~64M reads).

Contributing factors:
1. Sub-clonal heterogeneity: variants present in the source cell line but not in the ctDNA dilution
2. Panel coverage: 55/99 missing indels at 1M reads have n_ref = 0, indicating the target region is
   not captured by the panel at all
3. Repeat/homopolymer context: some indels fall in homopolymer runs where the alt breakpoint
   sequence is non-unique

## Resolution

The 100/170 target is not achievable with the current 15ng 2% sample regardless of algorithm
parameters. The data does not contain alt molecules for ~96/170 truth indels at 5M reads.

**Best achieved:** 74/170 = 43.5% with 150bp targets and 5M reads.

**To exceed this:** Requires a better ctDNA dilution dataset with more variants present in the
sample. All parameter axes have been exhausted: window size (100–200bp), k-mer size (21–31),
confidence threshold (0.0–0.80), read depth (1M to 5M). The junction k-mer ceiling at 5M reads
is 79/170; improving beyond that requires data with more variants present.

## Raw read analysis — missing indels

**Date:** 2026-04-19  
**Method:** Direct jellyfish query of junction k-mers against 1M-read and full-read R1 canonical
k-mer DBs. Junction k-mers are defined as `kmers(alt_seq, k=31) \ (kmers(ref_seq, k=31) | rc_kmers(ref_seq, k=31))`.
Repeated at k=31 (existing DBs: `kmers.jf` at 1M, `kmers_full.jf` at ~64M), k=25, and k=21.
Missing indels were identified by diffing truth_variants.norm.vcf against the called PASS/RESCUED/SUBTHRESHOLD set.
Scripts: `verify_ceiling.py`, `find_last4.py`, `recheck_allowlist.py`, `ceiling_full.py`.

### Coverage of missing variants

Reference-allele molecule counts from the baseline variants.tsv:

| Coverage category | Missing | Found |
|-------------------|---------|-------|
| n_ref = 0 (uncovered) | 55 | 0 |
| n_ref 1–49 (low) | 21 | 0 |
| n_ref 50–199 (medium) | 8 | 6 |
| n_ref 200+ (high) | 15 | 65 |

All 71 found indels have n_ref ≥ 85. 55 of the 99 missing indels have n_ref = 0 — the entire target
window has zero assembled molecules. These regions are either not captured by the panel, or the reads
are all filtered out before assembly. A further 21 have n_ref 1–49, placing them below
the sensitivity floor where even the reference allele is sparsely covered.

Coverage is systematically lower for missing variants: median n_ref = 0 (missing) vs 334 (found).

### Alt k-mer signal distribution

Max count of any single alt-specific k-mer, bucketed across all 99 missing indels:

| Max k-mer count | k=31 | k=25 | k=21 |
|-----------------|------|------|------|
| 0 | 94 | 93 | 93 |
| 1–5 | 4 | 5 | 5 |
| 6–10 | 0 | 0 | 0 |
| 11+ | 1 | 1 | 1 |

Shorter k-mers (k=25, k=21) expose one additional variant at the 1–5 hit level versus k=31,
but no new variants at meaningful signal levels. This confirms the prior finding that k-mer size
does not materially affect sensitivity for the missing indels.

### Variant-level findings

**chr10:43102447 Del(3bp) — FILTER ARTEFACT AT 1M, RECOVERED AT 5M**

All 26 junction k31-mers have 34–41 hits each in the 1M R1 reads (total 937 hits). Kam assembles
this as a Deletion with 12 alt molecules (2 duplex, 3 fwd, 7 rev), VAF=2.9%, confidence=1.0 at
1M reads. However, it is filtered as `StrandBias` (strand_bias_p = 0.0089, threshold = 0.01).

The strand bias is an artefact of locus-level sequencing asymmetry: the reference simplex reads are
72%/28% (fwd/rev), making the 3:7 alt split look biased by Fisher's exact test. A binomial test of
the alt reads alone gives p=0.34 — no bias in the alt read strand distribution. The 2 duplex alt
molecules provide strand-independent confirmation.

At 5M reads the call is PASS with 15 alt molecules (3 duplex, 6 fwd, 6 rev). The strand counts
balance as more reads arrive, removing the spurious filter. This confirms the 1M result was sampling
noise, not a true strand bias.

**Recommendation:** Bypass StrandBias for calls with ≥ 2 duplex alt molecules. Duplex support is
strand-independent evidence. Alternatively, require ≥ 20 simplex alt molecules before applying the
filter.

**chr11:108365362 Del(3bp) — WEAK SIGNAL AT 1M, RECOVERED AT 5M**

At 1M reads: one junction k31-mer has count 4 (approximately 2 alt reads), insufficient for
assembly. Kam reports 0 alt molecules. At 5M reads: all 29 junction k-mers are present with
count 2 each in the full reads (`kmers_full.jf`). Kam assembles 1 simplex alt molecule and the call
is PASS with VAF=0.55% (well below the 2% expectation). The effective VAF at this locus is ~0.5%,
not 2% — the variant is sub-threshold at 1M reads due to genuine allele fraction, not a filter bug.

**chr11:108301670 Del(8bp) — WEAK SIGNAL, 1 MOLECULE**

One k31-mer with 2 hits ≈ 1 alt molecule. Below any useful threshold.

**chr11:108327657 Del(7bp) — NOISE**

Four k31-mers each with exactly 1 hit. Scattered single-read signals across disjoint k-mers are
consistent with independent sequencing errors rather than a single molecule carrying the alt sequence.

**chr17:31235638, chr5:112792468 — NOISE (k=25 only)**

2 k25-mer hits, 0 k31-mer hits. Single-occurrence at lower k-sizes; likely sequencing errors that
share 25-mers with the alt sequence by coincidence or homopolymer context.

**chr7:55142293 — NOISE**

1 hit across all k-sizes. Single read; not a molecule.

**Remaining 93/99 missing indels — ZERO SIGNAL**

No alt k-mer hits at any k size. These variants are completely absent from the raw reads.

### Conclusions

1. **Two variants are recoverable with more reads.** chr10:43102447 was a StrandBias filter artefact
   at 1M reads (sampling noise in the 3:7 alt strand ratio); it passes at 5M reads with balanced
   strand counts. chr11:108365362 has genuine sub-2% VAF (~0.5%); it becomes detectable only at 5M
   reads where sufficient molecules accumulate.

2. **Four additional variants have marginal junction k-mer signal.** chr11:108301670 (1 k-mer,
   count 2), chr11:108327657 (4 k-mers, count 1 each), chr7:55142293 (1 k-mer, count 5 in full
   reads), chr11:108317461 (1 k-mer, count 1 in full reads). None of these represent real molecules
   after UMI dedup. No algorithm change can recover them without severe FP inflation.

3. **The remaining ~92/96 missing indels have zero junction k-mer signal.** These variants are not
   present in the ctDNA dilution sample at any detectable level.

4. **Coverage explains most of the gap.** 55/99 missing indels at 1M reads have n_ref = 0 —
   their target regions are not captured by the panel. A further 21 have n_ref < 50. Only 15/99
   have high coverage (n_ref ≥ 200), and of those only 1 (chr10:43102447) had actionable signal.

5. **Shorter k-mers provide no practical benefit.** k=21 and k=25 expose 1–2 additional variants at
   the 1–5 hit level but not at the assembly level.

6. **Junction k-mer vs flanking k-mer distinction is critical.** 43/170 variants have flanking
   k-mer evidence in jellyfish (shared with reference), but zero junction k-mer evidence. These
   cannot be detected by any assembly-based caller and inflate naive ceiling estimates. The true
   ceiling based on junction k-mers is 76/170 at 1M reads and 79/170 at 5M reads.

**Best achieved:** 74/170 = 43.5% at 5M reads, 150bp targets.
**True data ceiling:** 79/170 = 46.5% at 5M reads.
**Kam efficiency:** 74/79 = 93.7% of the achievable ceiling.
