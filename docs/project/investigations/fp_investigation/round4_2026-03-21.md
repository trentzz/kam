# FP Investigation — Round 4: min-family-size=2 as HUMID-equivalent filter
**Date:** 2026-03-21
**Config:** `--max-vaf 0.35 --min-family-size 2` (v6d) vs `--max-vaf 0.35` (v6a)

## Motivation

HUMID (used in the km/Jellyfish pipeline) reduces FPs by dropping singleton read families
before k-mer counting. kam defaults to `--min-family-size 1`, which keeps singletons as
valid molecules. A singleton molecule from a sequencing error contributes to `n_alt`. If
two such singletons hit the same genomic position (different UMIs, same coordinate), they
sum to n_alt=2 and pass the minimum threshold. Setting `--min-family-size 2` drops these
before the k-mer index is built, replicating HUMID's filter.

## Benchmark

All 24 samples, 2M read pairs, `--max-vaf 0.35 --min-family-size 2`.
Results: `benchmarking/results/tables_v6d/titration_results_2mreads.tsv`

## FP floor (0% VAF)

| Concentration | v6a FP | v6d FP | Δ FP |
|---------------|--------|--------|------|
| 5ng           | 50     | 35     | −15  |
| 15ng          | 86     | 61     | −25  |
| 30ng          | 96     | 72     | −24  |

Reduction: 29–30% of the noise floor is eliminated. The remaining 35–72 FPs per sample
are biological cfDNA variants (somatic mutations in normal tissue, clonal haematopoiesis,
common SNPs at reduced allele balance) — the same population described in round 3.

## 2% VAF results

| Sample | v6a sens | v6d sens | Δsens | v6a prec | v6d prec | Δprec |
|--------|----------|----------|-------|----------|----------|-------|
| 5ng    | 0.523    | 0.517    | −0.006 | 0.781   | 0.802    | +0.021 |
| 15ng   | 0.611    | 0.613    | +0.003 | 0.575   | 0.644    | +0.068 |
| 30ng   | 0.592    | 0.592    | 0.000  | 0.730   | 0.785    | +0.055 |

Precision improves substantially for 15ng (0.58→0.64) because 15ng had the highest
background call rate (169 FPs in v6a vs 127 in v6d at 2% VAF). Sensitivity is unchanged
within noise.

## 0.5% VAF sensitivity cost

| Sample | v6a sens | v6d sens | Δ     |
|--------|----------|----------|-------|
| 5ng    | 0.187    | 0.168    | −0.019 |
| 15ng   | 0.416    | 0.400    | −0.016 |
| 30ng   | 0.483    | 0.461    | −0.021 |

The sensitivity cost is 1.6–2.2pp at 0.5% VAF. This reflects genuine low-coverage variant
molecules that happened to be supported by only one read: dropping singletons removes some
real calls at the detection threshold. The cost is small relative to the precision gain.

## Why singletons inflate FPs

A singleton read family (one read, unique UMI) is assigned family_size=1. Under the
default `--min-family-size 1`, it becomes a molecule in the k-mer index. At low VAF,
variant-position singletons may outnumber true variant molecules: a given 100bp target
may have n_alt=2 composed entirely of two singleton error-derived molecules, not one
genuine duplex-confirmed molecule.

With `--min-family-size 2`, the molecule requires at least two reads with the same UMI
before being indexed. This eliminates PCR-free singletons, which are predominantly
sequencing errors in this chemistry (Twist duplex panels with PCR amplification). Genuine
variant molecules at reasonable VAF will accumulate multiple reads per UMI family after
PCR amplification.

## Recommendation

`--max-vaf 0.35 --min-family-size 2` is the recommended configuration for somatic ctDNA
calling. It reduces the FP floor by 15–25 per sample (29–30%) and improves precision at
2% VAF by 2–7pp, with a sensitivity cost of only 1.6–2.2pp at 0.5% VAF and essentially
no cost at 2% VAF.

The remaining 35–72 FPs per sample are biological and cannot be removed without orthogonal
evidence (matched normal, population allele frequency annotation).
